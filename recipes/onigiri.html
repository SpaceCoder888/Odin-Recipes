Onigiri

A Traditional Japanese Cuisine!

Onigiri, also known as omusubi, is a Japanese food made from white rice. It is oftenly wrapped around by nori.

Ingredients:

  • ⅔ cup short-grain rice, rinsed
  • ⅔ cup water
  • 3 sheets nori (dry seaweed), cut into 1-inch strips
  • salt
  • seasoned sesame seeds
  • shiso with hijiki (with dried seaweed flakes)

Recipe Instructions:

  1. Combine water and rice together in a pot, soak for half an hour.
  2. Cover the pot, heat it, lower the heat when the water is boiling.
  3. Keep cooking until the water is absorbed by the rice.
  4. Remove the heat, keep the pot covered for another 10 minutes or so.
  5. Remove the lid and allow the rice to cool off for 20 minutes
  6. Clean your hands, grab the rice and bend it into ball shapes.
  7. Wrap each onigiri with nori
  8. Season with salt, add sesame seeds and seaweed flakes
